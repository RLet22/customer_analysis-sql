# Customer Analysis (SQL Project)

SQL-based analysis of customer purchasing behavior focusing on retention, customer value, and purchasing patterns.

## Objective

The goal of this project is to analyze customer purchasing behavior and identify patterns related to customer value, retention, and purchasing frequency.

This analysis focuses on:

* Customer purchasing frequency
* Customer lifetime value (CLV)
* Customer retention and repeat purchases
* Customer segmentation
* High-value customers

---

## Dataset

The dataset used is the Olist Brazilian E-Commerce dataset, which contains customer, order, and payment information.

### Tables

* customers
* orders
* order_payments

### Key Columns

* customer_id — customer record identifier
* customer_unique_id — unique customer identifier
* order_id — order identifier
* order_purchase_timestamp — order purchase timestamp
* payment_value — payment amount associated with an order

---

## Data Grain

### customers

Each row represents a customer record.

### orders

Each row represents a single order.

### order_payments

Each row represents a payment associated with an order.

---

## Data Cleaning & Assumptions

To ensure accurate and meaningful analysis, the following rules were applied:

### Customer Definition

Used `customer_unique_id` as the business definition of a customer.

A single customer may have multiple `customer_id` values. `customer_unique_id` was used to represent the actual customer.

### Order Definition

Used `order_id` as the business definition of an order.

Each order was counted once when calculating customer purchasing behavior.

### Revenue Definition

Used `payment_value` for revenue calculations.

Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) and customer revenue metrics were calculated using `payment_value` from the `order_payments` table.

---

## Key Metrics

### Unique Customers

Count of distinct customers in the dataset.

### Orders Per Customer

Number of orders placed by each customer.

### Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

Total revenue generated by a customer across all purchases.

### First Purchase Date

Earliest purchase date recorded for each customer.

### Customer Recency

Most recent purchase activity for each customer.

### Days Since Last Purchase

Number of days between a customer's most recent purchase and the current date.

### Repeat Customers

Customers who placed more than one order.

### Repeat Customer Rate

Percentage of customers who placed more than one order.

### Customer Segmentation

Customers grouped by purchasing frequency:

* One-Time (1 Order)
* Repeat (2–4 Orders)
* Loyal (5+ Orders)

### Top Customers by Revenue

Highest-value customers based on total revenue generated.

## Analysis & Queries

All analysis was performed using SQL Server (T-SQL).

Key queries are organized in the `/sql` folder:

* `00_unique_customers.sql` — count unique customers
* `01_orders_per_customer.sql` — calculate orders per customer
* `02_customer_lifetime_value.sql` — calculate customer lifetime value
* `03_first_purchase_per_customer.sql` — identify first purchase date
* `04_customer_recency.sql` — customer recency analysis
* `05_recent_purchase_date.sql` — identify most recent purchase date
* `06_days_since_last_purchase.sql` — calculate days since last purchase
* `07_repeat_customers.sql` — identify repeat customers
* `08_repeat_customer_rate_pct.sql` — calculate repeat customer rate
* `09_segment_by_purchase_behavior.sql` — segment customers by purchasing frequency
* `10_top_10_customer_revenue.sql` — identify highest-value customers

---

## Key Findings

* Total Unique Customers: 96,096
* Repeat Customer Rate: 3.12%
* One-Time Customers: 93,099
* Repeat Customers (2–4 Orders): 2,978
* Loyal Customers (5+ Orders): 19
* Highest Customer Revenue: 13,664.08

### Business Insights

* Most customers purchased only once.
* Repeat customers represented a small percentage of the customer base.
* Very few customers demonstrated highly loyal purchasing behavior.
* Revenue generation was concentrated among a small group of high-value customers.
